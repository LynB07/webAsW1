How to assess a position in chess

It involves:Assessing the strengths and weaknesses of both sides, including material balance, king safety, pawn structure, and control of key squares or files.

How to assess the strengths and weaknesses

Look for weaknesses like open files, weak pawns around the king, or lack of defenders. A safe king is a strength; an exposed king is a weakness.

How to analyze tactics in a chess game

Identify immediate threats, combinations, and opportunities like forks, pins, or discovered attacks that can shift the game's outcome.

How to strategize in chess

Examine long-term plans based on pawn structure, piece placement, and control of open files or diagonals to achieve positional advantages.
